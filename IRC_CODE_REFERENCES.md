## 1. INCOME TAX BRACKETS

### 26 U.S.C. § 1 - Tax Imposed

**Primary Authority:** IRC § 1(a) through (d)

#### Single Filers - IRC § 1(c)
- Progressive tax brackets from 10% to 37%
- Tax is imposed on taxable income

#### Married Filing Jointly - IRC § 1(a)
- Separate bracket structure for joint returns
- Generally more favorable brackets

#### Married Filing Separately - IRC § 1(d)
- Generally half the brackets of joint filers
- Certain credits and deductions limited

#### Head of Household - IRC § 1(b)
- Intermediate brackets between single and joint
- Requires qualifying dependent

### 26 U.S.C. § 1(f) - Inflation Adjustments

**Authority:** IRC § 1(f)
- Tax brackets adjusted annually for inflation
- Based on chained Consumer Price Index (C-CPI-U)
- Adjustments announced by IRS in November of prior year

**IRS Guidance:**
- Revenue Procedure 2023-34 (2024 adjustments)
- Published annually in the Internal Revenue Bulletin

**2024 Tax Brackets (Rev. Proc. 2023-34):**

**Single:**
- 10% on income up to $11,600
- 12% on income $11,601 to $47,150
- 22% on income $47,151 to $100,525
- 24% on income $100,526 to $191,950
- 32% on income $191,951 to $243,725
- 35% on income $243,726 to $609,350
- 37% on income over $609,350

**Married Filing Jointly:**
- 10% on income up to $23,200
- 12% on income $23,201 to $94,300
- 22% on income $94,301 to $201,050
- 24% on income $201,051 to $383,900
- 32% on income $383,901 to $487,450
- 35% on income $487,451 to $731,200
- 37% on income over $731,200

## 2. STANDARD DEDUCTION

### 26 U.S.C. § 63 - Taxable Income Defined

**Primary Authority:** IRC § 63(c)

#### Standard Deduction Amounts

**IRC § 63(c)(2) - Basic Standard Deduction (2024):**
- Single: $14,600
- Married Filing Jointly: $29,200
- Married Filing Separately: $14,600
- Head of Household: $21,900

**IRC § 63(c)(4) - Limitation on Basic Standard Deduction:**
- Dependent's standard deduction limited
- Greater of $1,300 or earned income + $450 (max is regular amount)

### 26 U.S.C. § 63(f) - Aged or Blind Additional Amounts

**Authority:** IRC § 63(f)(1)

**Additional Standard Deduction for Age 65+:**
- Single: $1,950 (2024)
- Married (per person): $1,550 (2024)

**Additional Standard Deduction for Blind:**
- Same amounts as age 65+ additional amount
- Can claim both age and blind additional amounts

**Calculation Example:**
- Single, Age 67, Blind: $14,600 + $1,950 + $1,950 = $18,500

### 26 U.S.C. § 63(c)(4) - Inflation Adjustment

**Authority:** IRC § 63(c)(4)
- Standard deduction amounts indexed for inflation
- Updated annually based on C-CPI-U

## 3. SOCIAL SECURITY TAXATION

### 26 U.S.C. § 86 - Social Security Benefits

**Primary Authority:** IRC § 86

#### IRC § 86(a) - Inclusion in Gross Income

**General Rule:**
- Up to 50% or 85% of Social Security benefits may be taxable
- Based on "provisional income" calculation

#### IRC § 86(b) - Provisional Income Calculation

**Provisional Income = AGI + Tax-Exempt Interest + 50% of Social Security Benefits**

**Thresholds (NOT indexed for inflation):**

**Single Filers:**
- Below $25,000: 0% taxable
- $25,000 to $34,000: Up to 50% taxable
- Above $34,000: Up to 85% taxable

**Married Filing Jointly:**
- Below $32,000: 0% taxable
- $32,000 to $44,000: Up to 50% taxable
- Above $44,000: Up to 85% taxable

**Married Filing Separately:**
- Threshold: $0 (most benefits taxable)
- Up to 85% taxable

#### IRC § 86(c) - Formula for Determining Taxable Amount

**50% Tier Calculation:**
```
Taxable = Lesser of:
1. 50% of Social Security benefits, OR
2. 50% of (Provisional Income - Base Amount)
```

**85% Tier Calculation (if income exceeds higher threshold):**
```
Taxable = Lesser of:
1. 85% of Social Security benefits, OR
2. 85% of (Provisional Income - Adjusted Base Amount) + Lesser of:
   a. 50% of benefits, OR
   b. $4,500 (Single) / $6,000 (MFJ)
```

**IRS Publications:**
- Publication 915 - Social Security and Equivalent Railroad Retirement Benefits
- Worksheet for calculating taxable portion

## 4. RETIREMENT ACCOUNT CONTRIBUTIONS

### 401(k) Plans

#### 26 U.S.C. § 402(g) - Elective Deferrals

**Authority:** IRC § 402(g)(1)

**Annual Deferral Limit (2024):** $23,000

**IRC § 402(g)(5) - Catch-Up Contributions:**
- Age 50+: Additional $7,500 (2024)
- Total for age 50+: $30,500

**SECURE 2.0 Enhanced Catch-Up (Age 60-63):**
- IRC § 414(v)(2)(B)(iii) as amended
- Additional catch-up: $11,250 (2024) or 150% of regular catch-up
- Total for age 60-63: $34,250

#### 26 U.S.C. § 415 - Total Contribution Limits

**Authority:** IRC § 415(c)

**Total Contribution Limit (2024):**
- Lesser of $69,000 or 100% of compensation
- Includes employee + employer contributions
- With catch-up: $76,500 (age 50+)

**IRC § 415(d) - Cost-of-Living Adjustments:**
- Indexed annually for inflation
- IRS announces limits in October/November

### Traditional IRA

#### 26 U.S.C. § 408(a) - Individual Retirement Accounts

**Authority:** IRC § 408(a)(1)

**Contribution Limit (2024):** $7,000

**IRC § 408(d)(4) - Catch-Up Contributions:**
- Age 50+: Additional $1,000
- Total: $8,000

#### 26 U.S.C. § 219 - IRA Deduction Limits

**Authority:** IRC § 219(g)

**Phase-Out if Covered by Workplace Plan (2024):**

**Single:**
- Full deduction: AGI below $77,000
- Partial deduction: $77,000 to $87,000
- No deduction: Above $87,000

**Married Filing Jointly (both covered):**
- Full deduction: AGI below $123,000
- Partial deduction: $123,000 to $143,000
- No deduction: Above $143,000

**Married Filing Jointly (spouse not covered):**
- Full deduction: AGI below $230,000
- Partial deduction: $230,000 to $240,000
- No deduction: Above $240,000

### Roth IRA

#### 26 U.S.C. § 408A - Roth IRAs

**Authority:** IRC § 408A(c)(2)

**Contribution Limit:** Same as Traditional IRA ($7,000 + $1,000 catch-up)

#### IRC § 408A(c)(3) - Income Phase-Out Limits

**Authority:** IRC § 408A(c)(3)(B)

**Phase-Out Ranges (2024):**

**Single:**
- Full contribution: MAGI below $146,000
- Partial contribution: $146,000 to $161,000
- No contribution: Above $161,000

**Married Filing Jointly:**
- Full contribution: MAGI below $230,000
- Partial contribution: $230,000 to $240,000
- No contribution: Above $240,000

**Married Filing Separately:**
- Partial contribution: $0 to $10,000
- No contribution: Above $10,000

**Phase-Out Calculation:**
```
Reduced Limit = Max Contribution × [1 - (MAGI - Lower Threshold) / Phase-Out Range]
```

### Catch-Up Contributions

#### 26 U.S.C. § 414(v) - Catch-Up Contributions

**Authority:** IRC § 414(v)(1)

**General Rule:**
- Age 50+ eligible for catch-up contributions
- Applies to 401(k), 403(b), 457(b), IRA, SIMPLE plans

**SECURE 2.0 Enhancement:**
- IRC § 414(v)(2)(B)(iii) as amended
- Ages 60-63: Enhanced catch-up
- 150% of regular catch-up amount or $10,000 (inflation-adjusted)

## 5. REQUIRED MINIMUM DISTRIBUTIONS

### 26 U.S.C. § 401(a)(9) - Required Distributions

**Authority:** IRC § 401(a)(9)

#### IRC § 401(a)(9)(C) - Required Beginning Date

**Original Rule (before SECURE Act):**
- Age 70½ for those born before July 1, 1949

**SECURE Act (2019):**
- Age 72 for those born after June 30, 1949

**SECURE 2.0 Act (2022) - IRC § 401(a)(9)(C) as amended:**
- Age 73 for those born 1951-1959 (effective January 1, 2023)
- Age 75 for those born 1960 or later (effective January 1, 2033)

**Required Beginning Date:**
- April 1 of the year following the year you turn RMD age
- Subsequent years: December 31

#### IRC § 401(a)(9)(A) - Calculation Method

**Uniform Lifetime Table:**
- Treas. Reg. § 1.401(a)(9)-9
- Assumes beneficiary 10 years younger
- Used for most account owners

**Joint Life and Last Survivor Table:**
- Used if spouse is more than 10 years younger
- More favorable distribution period

**RMD Formula:**
```
RMD = Prior December 31 Account Balance / Life Expectancy Factor
```

**Life Expectancy Factors (Uniform Lifetime Table):**
| Age | Factor | Age | Factor | Age | Factor |
|-----|--------|-----|--------|-----|--------|
| 72  | 27.4   | 80  | 20.2   | 88  | 13.7   |
| 73  | 26.5   | 81  | 19.4   | 89  | 12.9   |
| 74  | 25.5   | 82  | 18.5   | 90  | 12.2   |
| 75  | 24.6   | 83  | 17.7   | 95  | 8.9    |
| 76  | 23.7   | 84  | 16.8   | 100 | 6.4    |
| 77  | 22.9   | 85  | 16.0   | 105 | 4.6    |
| 78  | 22.0   | 86  | 15.2   | 110 | 3.5    |
| 79  | 21.1   | 87  | 14.4   | 120 | 2.0    |

#### IRC § 408(a)(6) - IRAs Subject to RMD

**Traditional IRA:** Subject to RMD

**Roth IRA:** NOT subject to RMD during owner's lifetime
- IRC § 408A(c)(5) - No lifetime RMD requirement
- Beneficiaries must take distributions

**Roth 401(k):** Subject to RMD (unlike Roth IRA)
- Can rollover to Roth IRA to avoid RMDs

#### Still-Working Exception

**IRC § 401(a)(9)(C)(ii):**
- Applies to 401(k) and 403(b) plans
- Does NOT apply to IRAs
- Must not own 5% or more of company
- Plan must allow this exception

### 26 U.S.C. § 4974 - RMD Penalty

**Authority:** IRC § 4974(a)

**Original Penalty:** 50% of shortfall

**SECURE 2.0 Reduction:**
- IRC § 4974(a) as amended
- Reduced to 25% of shortfall (effective 2023)
- Further reduced to 10% if corrected within 2 years
- "Corrected" means taking the shortfall distribution

**Penalty Tax Calculation:**
```
Penalty = (Required RMD - Actual Withdrawal) × Penalty Rate
```

**Form 5329:**
- Used to report additional taxes on retirement plans
- Can request waiver for reasonable error

## 6. EARLY WITHDRAWAL PENALTIES

### 26 U.S.C. § 72(t) - 10% Additional Tax

**Authority:** IRC § 72(t)(1)

**General Rule:**
- 10% additional tax on early distributions
- Applies to distributions before age 59½
- In addition to regular income tax

#### IRC § 72(t)(2) - Exceptions to 10% Penalty

**Statutory Exceptions:**

**(A) Death** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(A)
- No penalty on distributions after death

**(B) Disability** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(A)(iii)
- Permanently and totally disabled
- Unable to engage in substantial gainful activity
- Expected to be long-continued or result in death

**(C) Substantially Equal Periodic Payments (SEPP)** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(A)(iv)
- Also known as "Rule 72(t)"
- Must continue for 5 years or until age 59½, whichever is longer
- Three approved methods:
  1. Required Minimum Distribution method
  2. Fixed Amortization method
  3. Fixed Annuitization method
- Modifying payments triggers retroactive penalty

**(D) Medical Expenses** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(B)
- Unreimbursed medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of AGI
- Only the excess amount is penalty-free

**(E) Health Insurance Premiums** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(D)
- IRAs only (not 401(k))
- While unemployed (received unemployment compensation for 12 weeks)
- During year of unemployment or following year

**(F) Higher Education** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(E)
- IRAs only
- Qualified higher education expenses
- For taxpayer, spouse, child, or grandchild

**(G) First-Time Home Purchase** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(F)
- IRAs only
- $10,000 lifetime limit
- Must be "first-time" (no home ownership in prior 2 years)

**(H) IRS Levy** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(A)(vii)
- Distributions due to IRS levy
- No penalty

**(J) Qualified Reservist Distributions** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(G)
- Called to active duty after September 11, 2001
- For at least 180 days

**(K) Qualified Birth or Adoption** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(H)
- Added by SECURE Act (2019)
- Up to $5,000 per birth or adoption
- Within 1 year of birth/adoption
- Can repay to avoid tax (but not required)

#### Rule of 55 - IRC § 72(t)(2)(A)(v)

**Authority:** IRC § 72(t)(2)(A)(v)

**Requirements:**
- Applies to 401(k) and 403(b) plans only
- Separation from service at age 55 or later
- Public safety employees: age 50 or later
- Must separate from service in or after year turning 55
- Does NOT apply to IRAs

**Special Rule for Public Safety Employees:**
- IRC § 72(t)(10)
- Age 50 instead of 55
- Must be public safety employee (police, firefighter, EMT, etc.)

#### SECURE 2.0 New Exceptions

**Terminal Illness** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(M) [Added 2023]
- Physician certification of terminal illness
- Likely to result in death within 84 months
- Can repay within 3 years

**Emergency Expenses** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(L) [Added 2024]
- Up to $1,000 per calendar year
- For unforeseeable or immediate financial needs
- Can only use once every 3 years (unless repaid)
- Self-certification allowed

**Domestic Abuse Victims** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(N) [Added 2024]
- Lesser of $10,000 or 50% of account
- Within 1 year of abuse
- Self-certification

**Federally Declared Disasters** - Various IRC sections
- Qualified disaster distributions
- Specific rules per disaster
- Can spread income over 3 years
- Can repay to avoid tax

### 26 U.S.C. § 72(t)(4) - Special Rules for Roth IRAs

**Authority:** IRC § 72(t)(4)

**Ordering Rule:**
1. Contributions withdrawn first (no tax, no penalty)
2. Conversions withdrawn next (taxed at conversion, no penalty if 5 years)
3. Earnings withdrawn last (taxed and penalized if not qualified)

**Qualified Distribution (no tax or penalty):**
- Account open 5+ years, AND
- Age 59½ or older, OR
- Death, OR
- Disability, OR
- First-time home purchase ($10,000 limit)

### 26 U.S.C. § 72(t)(6) - SIMPLE IRA Special Rule

**Authority:** IRC § 72(t)(6)

**Enhanced Penalty:**
- 25% penalty (instead of 10%)
- Applies if distribution taken within 2 years of first contribution
- After 2 years, normal 10% penalty applies

## 7. ROTH ACCOUNT RULES

### 26 U.S.C. § 408A - Roth IRAs

#### IRC § 408A(d) - Distribution Rules

**Authority:** IRC § 408A(d)(1)

**Qualified Distribution (no tax):**
- Account open 5+ years, AND
- One of the following:
  - Age 59½ or older
  - Death
  - Disability
  - First-time home purchase ($10,000)

**Non-Qualified Distribution:**
- Earnings subject to tax and 10% penalty
- Contributions always penalty-free

#### IRC § 408A(d)(3)(A) - Ordering Rule

**Distribution Order:**
1. Regular contributions (basis) - no tax, no penalty
2. Conversion contributions (FIFO) - no tax if 5 years, penalty if under 59½
3. Earnings - taxed and penalized if not qualified

#### IRC § 408A(e) - Roth Conversions

**Authority:** IRC § 408A(d)(3)

**Conversion Taxation:**
- Full amount taxable as ordinary income in year of conversion
- No 10% penalty on conversion itself
- Subject to 5-year rule for penalty-free withdrawal

**5-Year Rule for Conversions:**
- Each conversion has its own 5-year clock
- Prevents immediate penalty-free withdrawal of converted amounts
- Penalty applies if withdrawn before 5 years AND under 59½

**No Recharacterization:**
- Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (2017)
- Roth conversions are permanent (cannot reverse)

### Roth 401(k) - IRC § 402A

**Authority:** IRC § 402A

**Key Differences from Roth IRA:**
- Subject to RMD during owner's lifetime
- Can roll to Roth IRA to avoid RMDs
- No income limits on contributions
- Same contribution limits as traditional 401(k)

**Qualified Distribution Rules:**
- Same as Roth IRA
- 5-year rule starts from first contribution to any Roth 401(k)
- Contributions vs earnings tracked

## 8. HEALTH SAVINGS ACCOUNTS

### 26 U.S.C. § 223 - Health Savings Accounts

**Authority:** IRC § 223

#### IRC § 223(b) - Contribution Limits

**2024 Limits:**
- Individual: $4,150
- Family: $8,300
- Catch-up (age 55+): $1,000

**IRC § 223(b)(8) - Inflation Adjustment:**
- Indexed annually
- Rounded to nearest $50

#### IRC § 223(c) - High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)

**HDHP Definition (2024):**
- Minimum deductible:
  - Individual: $1,600
  - Family: $3,200
- Maximum out-of-pocket:
  - Individual: $8,050
  - Family: $16,100

#### IRC § 223(f) - Tax Treatment

**Contributions:**
- Tax-deductible (above-the-line deduction)
- Not subject to Social Security/Medicare tax if through payroll

**Distributions:**
- Tax-free if used for qualified medical expenses
- Taxable if not for qualified medical expenses

**Penalty:**
- 20% additional tax on non-qualified distributions
- Penalty waived after age 65, death, or disability
- Still taxable, but no penalty

#### IRC § 223(d) - Qualified Medical Expenses

**Authority:** IRC § 223(d)(2)

**Qualified Expenses:**
- Defined in IRC § 213(d)
- Medical, dental, vision care
- Prescription drugs
- Medical equipment
- Health insurance premiums (limited circumstances)
- Medicare premiums (Part B, Part D, Part C)
- Long-term care insurance (within limits)

**Not Qualified:**
- Over-the-counter drugs (without prescription)
- Cosmetic procedures
- General health items (vitamins, gym memberships)

#### IRC § 223(f)(7) - Last-Month Rule

**Authority:** IRC § 223(f)(7)

**Testing Period:**
- If HSA-eligible on December 1, can contribute full annual amount
- Must remain eligible through December 31 of following year
- Failure triggers recapture tax and penalty

## 9. ADDITIONAL TAXES

### Net Investment Income Tax

#### 26 U.S.C. § 1411 - Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)

**Authority:** IRC § 1411

**Tax Rate:** 3.8%

**Applies To:**
- Lesser of:
  1. Net investment income, OR
  2. Modified AGI exceeding threshold

**Thresholds (NOT indexed for inflation):**
- Single: $200,000
- Married Filing Jointly: $250,000
- Married Filing Separately: $125,000
- Head of Household: $200,000

**Net Investment Income Includes:**
- Interest, dividends, capital gains
- Rents, royalties
- Non-qualified annuities
- Passive business income

**Excluded:**
- Distributions from IRAs, 401(k)s (already subject to ordinary income tax)
- Tax-exempt municipal bond interest
- Active trade or business income

**Calculation:**
```
NIIT = 3.8% × Lesser of:
1. Net Investment Income
2. (MAGI - Threshold)
```

### Additional Medicare Tax

#### 26 U.S.C. § 3101(b)(2) - Additional Medicare Tax

**Authority:** IRC § 3101(b)(2)

**Tax Rate:** 0.9%

**Applies To:**
- Wages, compensation, self-employment income

**Thresholds (NOT indexed for inflation):**
- Single: $200,000
- Married Filing Jointly: $250,000
- Married Filing Separately: $125,000

**Unlike Regular Medicare Tax:**
- No employer match
- Only applies to employee
- Self-employed pay full 0.9%

**Withholding:**
- Employer withholds on wages over $200,000
- No coordination with spouse's income
- May need to make estimated tax payments

### Alternative Minimum Tax

#### 26 U.S.C. § 55 - Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)

**Authority:** IRC § 55

**Purpose:**
- Ensures high-income taxpayers pay minimum tax
- Parallel tax system

**Exemption Amounts (2024):**
- Single: $85,700
- Married Filing Jointly: $133,300
- Married Filing Separately: $66,650

**Phase-Out:**
- Begins at:
  - Single: $609,350
  - MFJ: $1,218,700
- Phase-out rate: 25 cents per dollar

**AMT Rates:**
- 26% on AMTI up to:
  - Single/MFJ: $232,600
  - MFS: $116,300
- 28% on AMTI above threshold

**Common AMT Triggers:**
- Large state/local tax deductions (limited under TCJA)
- Large long-term capital gains
- Incentive stock options (ISOs)
- Private activity municipal bonds
- High itemized deductions

**Retirement Planning Impact:**
- Roth conversions may trigger AMT
- Traditional IRA contributions reduce AMTI
- Should consider AMT in conversion planning

## 10. RECENT LEGISLATION

### SECURE Act (2019)

**Setting Every Community Up for Retirement Enhancement Act**

**Key Provisions:**

1. **RMD Age Increase** - IRC § 401(a)(9)(C)
   - Changed from 70½ to 72
   - Effective for those turning 70½ after December 31, 2019

2. **Elimination of Age Limit for IRA Contributions** - IRC § 219(d)(1)
   - Can contribute at any age (if working)
   - Previously prohibited after age 70½

3. **Birth or Adoption Distribution** - IRC § 72(t)(2)(H)
   - Up to $5,000 penalty-free
   - Within 1 year of event

4. **529 Plan to Roth IRA Rollovers** - IRC § 529(c)(3)(E)
   - Added in SECURE Act
   - Lifetime limit: $35,000
   - Account must be open 15 years
   - Subject to annual Roth IRA contribution limits

### SECURE 2.0 Act (2022)

**SECURE 2.0 Act of 2022**

**Signed into Law:** December 29, 2022

**Major Retirement Provisions:**

#### 1. Further RMD Age Increases

**IRC § 401(a)(9)(C) as amended:**
- Age 73 for those born 1951-1959 (effective January 1, 2023)
- Age 75 for those born 1960+ (effective January 1, 2033)

#### 2. Reduced RMD Penalty

**IRC § 4974(a) as amended:**
- Reduced from 50% to 25%
- Further reduced to 10% if corrected within 2 years
- Effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2022

#### 3. Enhanced Catch-Up (Ages 60-63)

**IRC § 414(v)(2)(B)(iii):**
- $10,000 or 150% of regular catch-up (whichever is greater)
- Indexed for inflation
- Effective 2025

#### 4. Catch-Up Must Be Roth for High Earners

**IRC § 414(v)(7):**
- If wages exceed $145,000 (indexed), all catch-up must be Roth
- Effective 2024 (delayed to 2026)
- Applies to 401(k), 403(b), 457(b)

#### 5. Employer Match on Student Loan Payments

**IRC § 401(m)(13):**
- Employers can match student loan payments
- Treated as elective deferrals for matching purposes
- Effective 2024

#### 6. Roth 401(k) RMD Elimination

**IRC § 401(a)(9) as amended:**
- Roth 401(k) and Roth 403(b) no longer subject to RMD
- Effective January 1, 2024
- Previously only Roth IRA was exempt

#### 7. Emergency Savings Accounts

**IRC § 402(a)(19):**
- Linked to retirement plans
- After-tax contributions up to $2,500
- First 4 withdrawals per year are penalty-free
- Automatic enrollment at 3% allowed
- Effective 2024

#### 8. Emergency Expense Distributions

**IRC § 72(t)(2)(L):**
- Up to $1,000 per year
- Self-certification
- Penalty-free
- Can repay within 3 years
- Once every 3 years unless repaid
- Effective 2024

#### 9. Terminal Illness Exception

**IRC § 72(t)(2)(M):**
- Physician-certified terminal illness
- Expected death within 84 months
- Penalty-free distribution
- Can repay within 3 years
- Effective 2023

#### 10. Domestic Abuse Victim Distributions

**IRC § 72(t)(2)(N):**
- Up to lesser of $10,000 or 50% of account
- Within 1 year of abuse
- Self-certification
- Can repay within 3 years
- Effective 2024

#### 11. Qualified Longevity Annuity Contracts (QLACs)

**IRC § 401(a)(9)(H) as amended:**
- Limit increased to lesser of $200,000 or 25% of account (previously $145,000 or 25%)
- 25% limit eliminated
- $200,000 indexed for inflation
- Effective 2023

#### 12. Automatic Enrollment

**IRC § 401(k)(13):**
- New 401(k) and 403(b) plans must auto-enroll
- Initial rate: 3% to 10%
- Automatic escalation to at least 10% (max 15%)
- Effective for plans established after December 29, 2022

#### 13. Part-Time Worker Eligibility

**IRC § 401(k)(2)(D) as amended:**
- Reduced from 3 years to 2 years of 500+ hours
- Effective 2025

#### 14. Increased Saver's Credit

**IRC § 25B as amended:**
- Effective 2027
- Becomes a matching contribution to retirement account
- Maximum credit: 50% of contribution (up to $2,000)

#### 15. 529 to Roth IRA Rollovers

**IRC § 529(c)(3)(E) (added by SECURE Act, refined by SECURE 2.0):**
- Lifetime limit: $35,000
- Account must exist 15+ years
- Contributions in last 5 years not eligible
- Subject to annual Roth IRA limits
- Effective 2024
